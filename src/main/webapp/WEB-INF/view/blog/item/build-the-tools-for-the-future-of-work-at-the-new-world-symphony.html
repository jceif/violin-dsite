Build the tools for the future of work at the New World Symphony

Paul Teyssier

It’s Monday. You wake up and scroll through numerous texts and emails, trying to set your priorities for the day. For the modern worker, that might look like getting to your desk and opening one or two dozen tabs, each one launching you into a different app: the one where your content lives, the one where your chats live, the one where your expense reports live, the one—you get the idea. One of those texts was relevant to one of those open windows, but who can remember? Those apps don’t speak to each other and besides, you’re absorbed in another app’s UI.

Now imagine editing content, responding to a customer service request, and completing a team review without once changing context—this is the future of work. Symphony is the starting point, a secure communications platform from which you manage your applications while maintaining compliance standards. The goal is to create a unified workflow in which a virtual interaction can equal or exceed a face-to-face one through context-rich, real-time communications. We’ve come a long way towards this goal but there’s still work to be done—that’s where you come in.

Symphony is building the collaboration platform of the future by partnering with the best innovators across the financial services industry. As we approach the Symphony Innovate 2016 conference, we’re taking the opportunity to host our developer community and give them space and resources to expand that ecosystem.

Symphony and the Symphony Software Foundation invite you to join top-notch financial services developers at “The New World Symphony” on October 5th in NYC for a day of coding and workshopping that gets us one step closer to the future of work. The New World Symphony is a developers-only event to complement the Symphony Innovate 2016 industry conference on October 6th. At Symphony Innovate, we’ll be sharing the ideas that drive the future of work. At the New World Symphony, you’ll be building the apps that make it a reality. The best apps to emerge from the New World Symphony will be formally recognized (and awarded) before an audience of industry leaders and journalists at Symphony Innovate.

What you’ll do:

Submit your team’s proposal by September 7th for a bot, integration, or app that uses Symphony to build a “New World” of work. Leverage existing projects or start new ones—we have limited capacity, so tell us your best ideas! If accepted (we’ll let finalists know by September 9th), you’ll develop and finish your project at our 1-day bootcamp.

Submit your proposal

What we’ll offer:

-Learn best practices, use cases, and hacks from product managers and engineers

-Network with fellow developers in the industry and discover some of the great solutions coming from the community

-Win a $5,000 donation to a charity of your choice, awarded to the most innovative project completed

-Winning projects will be recognized at the “Symphony Innovate 2016” keynote address on October 6

Submit your proposal, build an amazing bot, learn, and (hopefully) win—this is how we’ll start building the future of work. Good luck; we look forward to seeing your proposal!
