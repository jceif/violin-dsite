Choosing to care: why we gave $35,000 to charity last year

Kim Tran

For most of us, charity is a choice—each of us has their own relationship to generosity. But at Symphony, we have a saying: nothing is someone else’s problem. We feel a responsibility not just for what’s in front of us, but what’s around us. Because it’s all connected.

So I wasn’t at all surprised when my Symphony colleagues reached out to me with their own ideas for charity work, at the personal and the organization level. At this early stage in our company’s development (two years as of October), it’s important to all of us that Symphony define itself as a moral, forward-thinking organization with a culture of charitable giving.

In keeping with our core value that, “Many voices are louder than one,” our entire company pitched in for this to be a success. In April 2016, we introduced two employee-led philanthropy initiatives:

  #SymphonyGives creates opportunities for employees to donate money to charity;

  #SymphonyCares creates opportunities for employees to donate time and help to charity.

Once these initiatives yielded successful campaigns for organizations including Ronald McDonald House, East San Jose High School District, and Sports Relief (among others), the concept of charitable giving was now woven into our culture. It only made sense that our two major cultural and company events this year also became opportunities to continue in that spirit.

At Symphony Innovate 2016, our industry-wide conference and second anniversary, we hosted a successful hackathon that yielded two winning applications designed for the Symphony platform: a trending bot by developers from T. Rowe Price and a module state manager from FinTech Studios. Our prize to the winners? $10,000 worth of charitable contributions made on their behalf to organizations of their choice. We wanted to give something back while celebrating a successful year that exceeded our expectations.

Our 2016 global hiking challenge became another opportunity to give. I and our CEO, David Gurle, each donated $100 per hiker participating in our company-wide 14-mile hike. Turnout was exceptional with Symphonians from NYC, Palo Alto, APAC, and EMEA. Ultimately, Symphony raised $6,400 in donations for Project Transition USA and Circuelle Foundation.

Other charitable contributions made this past year include:

--More than 120 coats & over $2,500 to One Warm Coat

--125 pounds of food to Second Harvest Food Bank

--$350 to StandUp for Kids

--$1,000 to Operation Care & Comfort

--$2,000 to The White Helmets

--$1,000 to The Birthday Book Project

--$1,000 to Doctors Without Borders

--$1,000 to Yogi Divine Society

--In excess of $10,000 in individual employee contributions to their own favorite charities

All together, Symphony gave over $35,000 to charity in just one year (with a month to spare)! Coming up next, we’ll be planning a Winter Warmth Drive and a food drive for our global offices. Oh, and Movember, of course! Stay tuned for updates on all of the above.

Here’s to another generous year!
