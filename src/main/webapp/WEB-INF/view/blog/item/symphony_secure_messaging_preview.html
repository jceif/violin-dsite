Get a Sneak Peek of Symphony

David Gurle

How We’ve Improved Secure Business Communications for the 21st Century Workforce

Since our beta release on April 24th, we have received great feedback from our more than 30,000 beta users on the usability and functionality of Symphony.

As we move closer to our September 15th general availability release, I want to share a sneak peek of the upcoming preview release scheduled for late August. While we have implemented hundreds of improvements in the past few months, the main innovation is our new user interface. Our customers in beta will immediately notice how much we’ve made it easier to be productive, while offering powerful and rich features to protect their privacy and confidentiality of their messages.

Symphony is built from the ground up to help individuals, teams and businesses communicate from a single application, with security, compliance and privacy features built right in. Our new user interface design is constructed around three key areas: community, content and communication. Our aim is to help you and your workforce communicate efficiently and effectively.

Here is what is coming in our August preview release:

We’ve created an easy-to-use design that adapts to your needs

  • Our new interface has a crisp look and feel and allows you to choose your own background and color theme. We believe that how you communicate is personal by nature and we will continue to refine personalization features in Symphony to reflect your style and needs.
  • The new tool bar lets you post information to your followers, chat with anyone, filter messages with Signals, and blast a message to multiple places at once, including individuals or chat rooms in just one click.
  • An unlimited number of public or private chat rooms can be created inside or outside your organization and you can join open chat rooms which are relevant to you.

Single workspace for all your communications

  • With rich text editing, you can compose messages with the look and feel of an email or document, while including links, pictures, files and other content.
  • The communications dashboard is highly flexible, allowing you to open only one conversation or multiple conversations; monitor Signals all in one view; and power users can display Symphony on multiple screens.
  • Assign names and colors to your favorite or most important contacts, enabling you to quickly access your frequent or high priority conversations.
  • With one click, you can also view who received or read your messages in chat rooms.

Control the noise and filter by what you care about with Signals

  • We embrace tagging and handles just like on Twitter to make Symphony conversations richer and easier to surface: use the @ symbol to follow a user or to address them directly, and when they use it with you, you’ll get a notification.
  • In Symphony you can create your personalized filters by choosing which topics (#), securities ($) or users (@) you want to follow from within Symphony or from external news feeds, essentially forming your Signals you are interested in. Signals aggregates all of your personalized filters in one place for you to respond to your important information and share it, in one-click.
  • See what matters on the fly by having all the tags you use feed into a common dictionary that will intelligently suggest keywords or auto-complete names and topics you tag, building institutional knowledge for your company and team.

From desktop to mobile –Symphony goes where you go

  • Take your conversation anywhere, anytime by accessing an early preview of our iPhone* application.
  • Conversations using the Symphony app remain strictly confidential by securing access using Touch ID* or a personal pin code.

If you would like to join us for our upcoming preview, request an invite at get.symphony.com. Since Symphony was built for secure communication between groups and teams, the more people you invite to join you, the more productive you’ll be.

Once you have preview access, we welcome your feedback on Symphony using the hashtag #previewfeedback. You can also provide feedback using Twitter or Facebook.

All of this is just the beginning for Symphony. I can’t wait to share the other cool stuff our engineers are cooking. Stay tuned for more.
