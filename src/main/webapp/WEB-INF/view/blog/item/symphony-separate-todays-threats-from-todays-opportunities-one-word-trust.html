How Do You Separate Today’s Threats From Today’s Opportunities? One word: Trust.

David Gurle

In the digital workplace, the strength of your technology must match the strength of your relationships. With the speed and scale of disruption transforming our workplace, businesses increasingly rely upon each other to innovate.

We need to be part of a community which is able to collaborate in ways that does not put sensitive data at risk (neither ours nor that of our customers). It’s an ecosystem that must be built on circles of trust. Trust is the essence of any business transaction. Meanwhile, cyberattacks like the very recent Facebook breach involving 50 million user accounts are seeding doubt in people’s minds. With the multiplication of these incidents, many are questioning the strength and security of technology related to cloud-based services.

This is as real as it gets. That’s not just my view. Studies from the World Economic Forum and Harvard to EY and PwC all point to evidence that digital trust is declining just when we need it most.

My conversations with the top leaders of our customers around the world point to the same issue. We must embrace the disruption to get an edge. But can we really abdicate our supervision and trust the cloud providers with our data?

To address this issue some business leaders will attempt to put up more walls or turn away potential partners hence slowing down innovation. If so, they will ultimately lose their competitive edge.

When data stored in your collaboration platform powers your business, it’s unsettling, to say the least, to let it out of your control. And that’s exactly what you’re doing when you share it on popular messaging and collaboration platforms. Whether they are consumer solutions or business ones.

These are powerful tools but they are not as trustworthy as you think they are. These applications rely on the same set of technologies as Facebook does in order to attempt to protect your data. Unfortunately, we have just seen what happens when the cloud is exploited. Some marketing materials from those vendors go as far as claiming end-to-end encryption, but make no mistake, your message is decrypted as it’s processed in their data center, and this is how your data is put at risk. No amount of internal security processes at your company can change that. The risk is inherent in the architecture of traditional cloud-based collaboration solutions. If you trust these platforms to ward off all the efforts to assault them, then no need to worry. But facts tell us otherwise. Cyber-criminals are expected to steal 12 billion digital records this year, according to Jupiter Research. When you add in the 430 million types of malware that Forrester has identified online, and the assaults on banks, government agencies, and tech giants every day, safeguarding every piece of data could be a tough promise to keep.

But there are alternatives and businesses are finding trustworthy ways to collaborate. They are learning how collaboration leads to innovation. And they are learning how to reduce the risk of communicating sensitive data. Such solutions, like Symphony, rely on processing data only within the customer premises where data can be secured using strong true end to end encryption using key that are exclusively owned and managed by the customers.

For these reasons, the mood will be upbeat at Innovate 2018, Symphony’s premier customer and partner event. The New York event, our third since the launch of Symphony, will bring together over 500 executives, IT professionals, journalists, and thought leaders to talk about the impact that secure collaboration – along with machine learning and automated workflows – can have on the new digital workplace.

To build trust, you need both transparency and technology that keeps data away from anything that can be hacked. For the next generation of collaboration, Symphony offers access to a trusted business network that enables customers to carry out mission critical business transactions. Want to organize a get together with your team? Think about using Teams or consider Slack. Want to execute a billion-dollar transaction? You can trust Symphony.

In the new digital workplace, the most powerful technologies will not be the machines that replace people, but the tools that enhance their ability to bring value to their customers. Anything that comes between those relationships is a potential threat on both sides. Data is critical, and trust is such a fragile commodity. Losing trust is a risk no business leader can afford.
