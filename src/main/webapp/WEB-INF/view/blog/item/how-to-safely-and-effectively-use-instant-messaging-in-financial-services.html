How to Safely and Effectively Use Instant Messaging in Financial Services

Queenie Chan

按此浏览中文版本

The way we work is changing rapidly. As many business owners are looking to digitize internal workflows and improve customer service with bots, it is not surprising to see instant messaging (IM) accounts are expected to grow 8% annually, to 8.6 billion world-wide in four years.1 These new IM systems are user-friendly and convenient, but are they equipped to protect sensitive client data shared in regulated industries like financial services?

Recently, the Hong Kong Securities and Futures Commission (SFC) issued guidance on the use of instant messaging applications in financial services, specifically when receiving orders from clients, around the following areas:

1. Centralised record keeping

2. Security and reliability

3. Compliance monitoring

4. Internal policies and procedures

5. Client awareness

How should you and your company react to this guidance? Look for an instant messaging application that does not only meet these guidelines, but was built from the ground up to empower your organization’s productivity while giving you full ownership of your data. Symphony does just that.

1. Centralised record keeping

The SFC recommends that all messages - regardless of device - relating to client orders should be secured, maintained, and recorded for no less than 2 years.

Symphony messages - sent via desktop or mobile - are secured with unique and state of art end-to-end encryption with an option for customer-owned key infrastructure for our Enterprise customers. Client data can be recorded, maintained, and exported for as long as our customers need. This data can be fed directly into our client’s record retention systems, which allows them to comply with global regulations and compliance requirements without external dependency.

2. Security and reliability

The SFC guidelines call for client identity validation, protection against unauthorized data or account access, and secure external communications. They continue: “safeguards should cover end-to-end data transmission as well as the IM accounts and devices used for storing and processing the order messages.”

On client identity validation, Symphony’s Global Directory is the only customer validated repository of trusted identities. We clearly indicate who is validated in our directory through verified user badges. Banks know they are servicing the right contacts and clients have the peace of mind that they are sharing sensitive data with designated representatives.

To protect against unauthorized data or account access, Symphony was built with end-to-end encryption as its foundation, not an add on, to protect data through the entire transmission lifecycle.

For secure external communications, Symphony provides fully encrypted external communication capabilities, meaning all Symphony users can securely communicate and collaborate across company boundaries. This workflow is validated daily by the growing Symphony network, consisting of 320,000 users across 290 companies to date.

3. Compliance monitoring

Compliance is key in regulated industries, and the SFC highlights the need for instant messaging applications to provide access to data for monitoring, compliance reviews, and the ability to flag unusual transactions.

Symphony goes beyond these suggested guidelines. We offer fully customizable active and passive compliance features such as audit trails, information barriers, expression filters, and conversation monitoring in real time by client’s compliance teams. Our customers have implemented Symphony’s compliance standards as the new default requirements for their communication tools, further confirming Symphony as the leader of this domain.

4. Internal policies and procedures

Another recommendation by the SFC is that only select employees should have access to sensitive client orders, and should be trained to execute them quickly and accurately.

Symphony allows selected administrators to assign user-level feature entitlements that mediate who employees can talk to inside and outside their organization, as well as control what data they can access. These granular yet user-friendly permissions allow your organization to safeguard policies and mitigate the security & compliance risks from human errors when using chat systems. For example, Symphony allows selected managers to check any message before it is sent, to ensure sensitive files and data are transmitted to the right contact.

5. Client awareness

In a technological world, transparency is more important than ever. The SFC advises that clients should be aware of how their orders are being executed, what platforms are being used, and the security of their content.

Symphony Enterprise customers have the option to maintain all content on-premise, choose which employees to deploy and how, and hold the encryption keys to their own data. This way, Enterprise customers know exactly how their data is being used and can answer client inquiries quickly.

Symphony also supports bots and workflow automation, which allows companies to augment productivity by integrating with internal business systems, third-party content providers, and existing trading systems. For example, a large sell-side institution has built a bot to keep its clients and employees informed in real time about their order transaction status as it is executed.

Symphony is excited that the SFC has published guidance around the use of instant messaging applications to bring awareness to the need for data security, compliance, and transparency. The Symphony platform will continue to develop and support these market needs as they evolve.
