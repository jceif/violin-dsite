Five Questions Insurance Companies Need to Ask About Collaboration

Chad Roth

Every collaboration with a new person is like when you take a bin of Legos and dump them on the floor. All of the pieces to work with are right there. A floor full of Legos is full of possibilities. --Ricky Reed

The insurance industry is built on the potential of relationships -- those connecting agents, brokers, clients, and carriers. However, in today’s competitive business environment, traditional connectivity isn’t enough. Once you are connected with a counterparty, relationships get stronger when effective collaboration happens.

Due to the digital transformation underway across the insurance sector, the way organizations collaborate continues to evolve across clients of all sizes. This is true for brokers, carriers, and newer insurtech entrants. Five questions that insurance companies should be asking about collaboration include:

  1. Is traditional connectivity enough to achieve business goals and move the dial?
  2. Is connecting with N+1 more brokers or underwriters going to help you achieve your goals and if so how?
  3. How can relationships be codified into real-time collaboration?
  4. What are the security and compliance considerations of collaboration?
  5. How do you make collaboration efficient both inside your firm and also firm to firm?

Connectivity is important and necessary but falls short of what is really needed to bring clients the best solutions - collaboration. This thought has been echoed in a recent keynote given by Pina Albo, the CEO of the Hamilton Group at the Insurance Insider New York conference that took place in mid-June 2018. Secure, real-time collaboration between agents and carriers is what will truly allow the insurance industry to innovate faster and match critical client needs. Connectivity is commoditized, but collaboration is a true differentiator -- and one that all partners value.

How specifically does the industry hope to collaborate?

Ernst & Young published a paper in 2017, ’The Insurance Agent of the Future’, in which it surveyed 530 insurance agents to gain a better understanding of marketplace trends. They learned that agents are looking for “simple customer and agent experiences, which in turn will drive agent loyalty.” Simply put, agents are looking to fill out fewer forms and improve online tools. Digital onboarding, a frequent use case for Symphony, was also mentioned as one of the areas needing improvement. At their core, agents want to realize operational efficiencies, so they can spend their time expanding their businesses.

Another major trend listed was “Agents see close collaboration with carriers as driving future growth.” Agents are looking for more meaningful relationships with underwriters. They want the underwriters to take on more solutions-based roles, as opposed to transactional roles. The underwriting function is at a crossroads. While there are initiatives underway to streamline underwriting, there is also a strong need for this closer 1:1 mapping between agents and underwriters, especially for smaller size clients.

As more and more carriers look to automate their underwriting to gain operational efficiencies and speed, underwriters do not become obsolete – they simply become more available to spending time working with agents and clients on innovative new solutions. Another question to ask is: how do you utilize secure, real-time collaboration to optimize that underwriter - agent interaction?

While the EY paper focuses on agents, it is reasonable to extrapolate that agents and brokers are seeking similar abilities. Both groups alike want to collaborate with carriers in order to increase their revenue while at the same time reducing administrative burdens.

Given the high price of client and broker acquisition, it makes sense for carriers to concentrate their resources on how they can best collaborate with their existing distribution partners. Secure collaboration can enable growth and a provide sustainable competitive advantage. The importance of this collaboration amplifies when it comes to MGAs, managing general agents, who have a greater need to efficiently share data and systems.

Like the bucket of Lego pieces on the floor, there are endless opportunities as agents and brokers transform how they do business. But by snapping the pieces together, the whole becomes greater than the sum of the parts. And secure, real-time collaboration can help make sure the right pieces are connected and communicating.
