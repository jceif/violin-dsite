A New Category of Communication | Taking a Step Away from Messaging Apps

David Gurle

Communication is the human connection. Whether it’s in our personal or professional lives, through words or images, with happy or sad emotions, we depend on communication to strengthen our relationships, share our thoughts, and organize our lives.

Given how critical communication is to everything we do, it is no surprise that we are very keen on improving our connections with our network. This appetite has led us to use an increasing number of communication tools and messaging apps to get our points across.

Email, instant messaging, SMS, mobile messaging apps, unified communications, voice and video over IP, audio and video conferencing, web conferencing, chat rooms, group messaging, forums, tweets, and social networks are all part of our personal and professional lives these days.

As I contemplate this new era, I wonder whether this communication application “creep” is for better or for worse.

On the one hand, my life looks better – I can access people I care about more easily and quickly, with some functionality that mimics a face-to-face conversation. Mobile devices, texting, and instant messaging are the cornerstones of this faster, more accessible communication. I can send my kids a picture of where I am, my colleagues a memo on our meeting, and my friends a reminder of where to meet for dinner in seconds. And small things like emoticons allow me to communicate my feelings in a faster, yet deeper, level than text alone.

On the other hand, all these tools make it harder to find and share information – and ultimately leave me feeling frustrated, disorganized and unproductive. I exchange messages with my colleagues, friends, and family on several messaging apps that at the end of the day, just results in scattered information. Where was that link my colleague sent me – in my email or in the instant message? Should I text my wife or email her – which one will she see first? How am I going to maintain the thread of my team’s dialogue – how can everyone contribute to conversation efficiently? I have come to accept, albeit reluctantly, that information we have shared and moments we have cherished have become ephemeral and disorganized.

Surely I can’t be alone in experiencing the joys and frustrations of this new era in which we live.

These frustrations have been the feeding ground for many start-ups to create variations of different messaging apps. For any given communication mode we have scores of competing products with more or less the same features. Unfortunately, the explosion of these tools has become a bane rather than a helpful feature of our lives.

With the exponential increase of inbound messages, regardless of which application they come from, our notification centers, contact lists and inboxes have become increasingly unmanageable. Most have pinned email at the root of our collective frustration. Unfortunately, each new collaboration tool, enterprise social network, messaging application, or unified communication platform has only added one more place for us to collect information, rather replace our inbox and help us digest and make use of our communications.

So at Symphony, we have decided to take a very different approach. We are not waging a war against email; rather, we are embracing the best of what it has to offer. Email is universal. It has reach that only the telephone network can beat, it allows its users to structure and organize information, and it enables any type of data to be exchanged.

We see an opportunity to create something different. We are not building yet another venue for the current universe of information we produce and consume.

We are building a product that embraces the best of all our disparate communication tools — delivering interactivity, speed, and reliability.
We are building a platform that makes our digital interactions more meaningful, intimate, powerful, and productive.
We are building a way to experience moments as if we are together in the same place sharing our emotions, thoughts, and ideas.
We are building a new product category that in the coming months will reveal itself…And it will make our communications more human…
“Secure, Seamless Communication”
