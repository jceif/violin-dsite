Symphony Launches First Integrations

Paul Teyssier

It is now possible for users to bring even more of their day-to-day workflow into Symphony. Our initial integrations - GitHub, JIRA, Salesforce, the Universal Webhook and Zapier - are available to all Symphony customers in the Symphony Market today.

This is a result of the availability of our open-source Integrations framework, which makes it possible to deliver content and notifications from third-party and in-house applications directly into the Symphony platform. We plan to add more to these initial integrations soon - so stay tuned!

What are the benefits of these integrations?

GitHub allows users to track changes of key software projects in Symphony, including pull requests, code pushes, issues, comments, merges, and the latest deployment status.

JIRA can notify users on Symphony when their name is mentioned in a ticket, when a critical bug is filed and more. This integration uses personalized rules and also maps JIRA labels to Symphony signals, automatically prioritizing critical information.

Salesforce helps business development teams stay up to date on their pipeline. The integration advises users when someone changes the status of an opportunity in Salesforce.

Symphony’s Universal WebHook enables users to connect third-party or in-house software applications that support industry-standard webhooks technology to send messages within Symphony.

Zapier adds an ecosystem of 750+ apps to the Symphony platform. Zapier sends notifications and content to Symphony chats or rooms from popular applications including Gmail, Office 365, Trello, HubSpot, Twitter, LinkedIn, and hundreds of other productivity apps.

Want to learn more about Symphony integrations or how to bring them into your platform? Visit the Symphony Market.

Finally, thank you to all customers for providing us feedback during our beta period!

Note: Most Symphony customers will see Symphony Integrations in the Symphony Market today. For our largest Enterprise customers, the availability of Integrations is controlled by internal system administrators and will be customized based on company-specific preferences. If you don't see the Integration you want, talk to your Symphony administrator!
