Wiring the Big Apple (Securely)

Karen Brooks

Home to 8.5 million, NYC is the place for connections. But when you’re in the City that Never Sleeps, finding wifi and keeping your phone fully charged can be a pain. A former consultant, I’ve been in the position many times of crisscrossing the city from meeting to meeting, desperate to quickly find a phone charger or connect to wifi.

This is why LinkNYC's January 2016 installation of free public wifi and charging stations is an exciting development for New Yorkers and tourists alike. The 7,500 hubs designed by CityBridge are planned for rollout across all5 boroughs, and will be a welcome oasis for anyone thirsting for reception in a dead zone desert. But as with most things the world of technology, added convenience comes with some element of increased security risk.

As cities, parks and airports roll out similar programs across the country, it’s a good time for reminders of how to use these public services safely:


Security Risk: Skimming

Just like ATM and credit card scams, USB "skimmers" could be put in place of the USB chargers, putting nasty malware on your phone or device.

How you can protect yourself

Your best defense is to inspect the USB port before using it. Does it look off? Go to another hub.

Check out these skimmer images so you know what to look for.


Security Risk: Sniffing

Because the network is open to the public, a malicious person could monitor the data you transmit while connected to any unsecured website (userIDs, passwords, etc.).

How you can protect yourself

Just accessing the public bus timetable? Go for it, stress free.

Plan to login with your user credentials, share credit card info or other personal information? Make sure you’re browsing on secure websites (with an “https” in the address).

If the hub offers it,choose a private connection option. Find more great security tips here.


Security Risk: Spoofing

A network can be “spoofed”when a hacker renames his bad network to the same name as the good, publicWi-Fi network. If you select the bad network, this enables the hacker to watch your network activity and steal your information.

How you can protect yourself

Always turn off FileSharing and wifi when you’re not using it. Your device will automatically try to reconnect to past network names, and won’t know the difference between the bad network and the good one.
